# Dracula

## Overview
*Dracula* is a gothic horror masterpiece chronicling the attempt by a group of friends and allies to stop the vampire Count Dracula from spreading his undead curse from Transylvania to England.

## Main Characters
- **Jonathan Harker**: English solicitor and initial captive of Dracula.
- **Mina Harker (née Murray)**: Jonathan’s devoted wife.
- **Dr. Abraham Van Helsing**: Dutch scientist and vampire hunter.
- **Count Dracula**: The ancient, cunning, and powerful vampire antagonist.
- **Lucy Westenra**: Mina’s doomed friend and early victim.
- **John Seward, Arthur Holmwood, Quincey Morris**: Dracula’s adversaries and Mina’s protectors.

## Plot Summary
Jonathan Harker visits Transylvania to help Dracula purchase property in England, only to discover his host’s supernatural menace. After returning home, a series of mysterious events in England—including Lucy’s decline and Mina’s peril—clarifies Dracula’s threat. Van Helsing leads the group’s efforts to hunt and defeat Dracula. In a climactic pursuit, Dracula is destroyed in Transylvania, ending his reign of terror.

## Themes
- **Good vs. Evil**: The fight between faith, reason, and supernatural evil.
- **Modernity vs. Ancient Superstition**: Science and old-world beliefs clash.
- **Sexuality, Gender, and Power**: Subtle tensions reflect Victorian anxieties.

## Literary Significance
Dracula helped cement vampire lore in Western culture and remains a profound exploration of fear, identity, and the boundaries of science and superstition.
