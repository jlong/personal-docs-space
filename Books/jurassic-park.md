# Jurassic Park

It's one of Michael Crichton's best books. Published in 1990, it explores the dangers of genetic engineering and the hubris of trying to control nature. The story follows paleontologist Dr. Alan Grant and mathematician Dr. Ian Malcolm as they tour a theme park populated by cloned dinosaurs on a remote island off Costa Rica.

They made a movie based on it. Spielberg directed the 1993 film adaptation, which became a massive box office hit and spawned an entire franchise. The film's groundbreaking use of CGI dinosaurs revolutionized visual effects in cinema and earned three Academy Awards for its technical achievements.

## Key Themes

- The unpredictability of complexify systems (chaos theory) — embodied by Ian Malcolm's warnings that the park's systems would inevitably fail

- Science without ethical boundaries — John Hammond's team resurrects extinct species without considering the consequences

- The illusion of control over nature — the park's elaborate containment systems prove wholly inadequate against living, evolving organisms
