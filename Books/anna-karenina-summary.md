# Anna Karenina

## Overview
Leo Tolstoy’s **Anna Karenina** explores the fateful affair of Anna, a married aristocrat, and Count Vronsky, as their love collides with rigid Russian social codes.

## Main Characters
- **Anna Karenina**: Beautiful and sensitive, Anna is torn between desire and duty.
- **Alexei Alexandrovich Karenin**: Anna’s dignified but emotionally cold husband.
- **Count Vronsky**: A passionate cavalry officer who becomes Anna’s lover.
- **Konstantin Levin**: A landowner whose philosophical struggles ground the novel.
- **Kitty Shcherbatsky**: A young woman whose own journey to happiness contrasts Anna’s fate.

## Plot Summary
Anna meets Vronsky while traveling to Moscow; their chemistry is immediate and dangerous. Defying convention, Anna leaves her husband and son for Vronsky, facing social ostracism and personal remorse. Their love, initially intoxicating, is eroded by jealousy and isolation. Meanwhile, Levin’s pursuit of happiness through work, marriage to Kitty, and spiritual awakening offers a counterpoint.

As Anna’s relationship with Vronsky deteriorates and her alienation deepens, she makes a tragic choice by ending her own life. Levin’s peace, hard-won through faith and family, endures.

## Themes
- **Desire vs. Duty**: The conflict between personal happiness and social expectation.
- **Society and Ostracism**: How rigid morality punishes those who defy conventions.
- **Faith and Meaning**: Levin’s search for ethical and spiritual fulfillment.
- **Love’s Redemptions and Ruin**: The contrasting fates of Anna/Vronsky and Levin/Kitty.

## Literary Significance
Regarded as one of the greatest novels ever written, Anna Karenina’s insights into love, morality, and the human psyche continue to resonate.
