# The Scarlet Letter – Novel Summary

Set in 17th-century Puritan Boston, The Scarlet Letter follows Hester Prynne, who bears the stigma of an embroidered scarlet “A” after conceiving a child out of wedlock. While Hester endures public shame, she quietly raises her daughter, Pearl, and offers compassion to the community she’s ostracized from. Meanwhile, Reverend Dimmesdale—Pearl’s secret father—grapples with guilt and physical decline, and Hester’s estranged husband, disguised as Roger Chillingworth, seeks vengeance. Hawthorne explores themes of sin, redemption, and the tension between individual identity and societal moralism, culminating in Hester’s resilience and Dimmesdale’s tragic confession.
