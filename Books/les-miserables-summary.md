# Les Misérables

## Overview
*Les Misérables* traces the spiritual journey of Jean Valjean, an ex-convict seeking moral renewal amid poverty, love, and revolution in 19th-century France.

## Main Characters
- **Jean Valjean**: An ex-convict who transforms through acts of compassion.
- **Javert**: A dogged police inspector obsessed with law and duty.
- **Fantine**: A victim of circumstance whose suffering inspires Valjean’s redemption.
- **Cosette**: Fantine’s daughter, adopted and cherished by Valjean.
- **Marius Pontmercy**: A revolutionary and Cosette’s lover.
- **Éponine, Gavroche**: Figures representing the pain and hope of the poor.

## Plot Summary
Paroled after nineteen years in prison, Valjean struggles against a prejudiced society and the relentless pursuit of Javert. Moved by the Bishop’s mercy, he dedicates his life to virtuous deeds, protecting Fantine and her daughter, and playing a father to Cosette. Themes of justice and mercy play out against the backdrop of the Paris Uprising of 1832, where Marius joins the fighting. Ultimately, Javert’s moral rigidity destroys him, and Valjean’s example inspires hope for love and redemption.

## Themes
- **Redemption and Compassion**: The power of forgiveness.
- **Justice vs. Law**: The conflict between mercy and rigidity.
- **Social Injustice**: Poverty, class, and systemic oppression.
- **Revolution and Hope**: The collective struggle for a better future.

## Literary Significance
Enduring for its powerful depiction of human struggle and idealism, *Les Misérables* is a foundational text exploring the great questions of justice, society, and compassion.
