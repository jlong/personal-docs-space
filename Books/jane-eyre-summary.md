# Jane Eyre - Novel Summary

Jane Eyre, written by Charlotte Brontë, is a coming-of-age novel that follows the life of its eponymous heroine as she navigates a world defined by social class, gender roles, and personal morality. Orphaned as a child, Jane is raised by her cruel Aunt Reed before being sent to the harsh Lowood School. There, she learns resilience and kindness amid adversity. As a young woman, Jane becomes a governess at Thornfield Hall, where she meets the enigmatic and brooding Mr. Rochester.

Despite their vastly different social positions, Jane and Rochester develop a deep emotional connection. However, their happiness is jeopardized when Jane discovers that Rochester harbors a dark secret: his wife, Bertha Mason, is still alive but mentally ill and hidden in the attic. Shattered, Jane chooses to leave Thornfield and Rochester, prioritizing her integrity and self-respect.

Alone and destitute, Jane is taken in by the Rivers family, where she discovers newfound relatives and inherits a fortune. Ultimately, Jane's strong will, compassion, and commitment to her principles guide her back to a now-blinded and repentant Rochester. In the end, Jane and Rochester marry as equals, and the novel closes on their mutual happiness and the triumph of inner worth over social convention.
