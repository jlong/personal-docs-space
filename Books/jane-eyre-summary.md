# Jane Eyre

## Overview
*Jane Eyre* follows the struggles and triumphs of its orphan heroine, who overcomes cruelty, isolation, and adversity with dignity and integrity.

## Main Characters
- **Jane Eyre**: The plain but fiercely independent protagonist.
- **Edward Rochester**: Mysterious Thornfield Hall master and Jane’s great love.
- **St. John Rivers**: A distant cousin and missionary, offering Jane a different path.
- **Mrs. Reed, Bertha Mason**: Women who in different ways shape Jane’s destiny.

## Plot Summary
Orphaned, Jane suffers neglect from the Reeds and hardship at Lowood School, but ultimately finds a position as governess at Thornfield Hall. A passionate romance blossoms between Jane and Edward Rochester, but Jane flees upon discovering he is married to the insane Bertha Mason. After enduring privation, Jane learns she’s inherited wealth and family. In the end, she reunites with a blinded, humbled Rochester, marrying him on her own terms.

## Themes
- **Independence and Integrity**: Jane’s commitment to self-respect and autonomy.
- **Gender and Social Class**: The limitations Jane faces—and triumphs over.
- **The Power of Love and Forgiveness**: Redeeming relationships.

## Literary Significance
A revolutionary work for its time, *Jane Eyre* established the passionate inner life of the female protagonist and remains a touchstone of feminist and romantic literature.
