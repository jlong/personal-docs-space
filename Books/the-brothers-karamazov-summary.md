# The Brothers Karamazov

The Brothers Karamazov explores the moral, spiritual, and psychological struggles of the three Karamazov brothers—Dmitri, Ivan, and Alexei—whose lives converge around the murder of their tyrannical father, Fyodor. Set against 19th-century Russia’s social and religious tensions, the novel grapples with questions of faith, doubt, free will, and the nature of evil. Through courtroom drama, mysticism, and philosophical dialogue, Dostoevsky crafts an epic meditation on redemption and human responsibility, culminating in a powerful testimony to the enduring quest for truth and compassion.
