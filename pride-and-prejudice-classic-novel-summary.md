# Pride and Prejudice - Classic Novel Summary

## Overview

**Author:** Jane Austen  
**Published:** 1813  
**Genre:** Romance, Social Commentary, Comedy of Manners  
**Setting:** Regency-era England, early 19th century

## Plot Summary

Pride and Prejudice follows the complex relationship between Elizabeth Bennet, a witty and independent young woman, and Fitzwilliam Darcy, a wealthy but seemingly arrogant landowner. Set in rural England, the story explores themes of love, marriage, social class, and personal growth.

### Main Characters

- **Elizabeth Bennet**: The intelligent, spirited protagonist who initially misjudges Darcy
- **Fitzwilliam Darcy**: A wealthy landowner who appears proud but is actually honorable
- **Jane Bennet**: Elizabeth's gentle, kind-hearted older sister
- **Charles Bingley**: A wealthy, cheerful gentleman who falls for Jane
- **Mr. and Mrs. Bennet**: Elizabeth's parents - he's witty but detached, she's silly and marriage-obsessed
- **Mr. Wickham**: A charming soldier who deceives Elizabeth about Darcy
- **Mr. Collins**: A pompous clergyman and the Bennet family's heir

### Key Events

1. **First Impressions**: Elizabeth and Darcy meet at a ball where he snubs her, leading to mutual dislike
2. **Wickham's Lies**: Elizabeth believes Wickham's false claims about Darcy's character
3. **Darcy's First Proposal**: Darcy proposes to Elizabeth, who rejects him due to his apparent pride and supposed mistreatment of Wickham
4. **The Letter**: Darcy writes Elizabeth a letter explaining his actions and revealing Wickham's true character
5. **Lydia's Elopement**: Elizabeth's youngest sister elopes with Wickham, threatening the family's reputation
6. **Darcy's Intervention**: Darcy secretly arranges and pays for Lydia and Wickham's marriage
7. **Second Proposal**: Elizabeth and Darcy, having overcome their pride and prejudice, become engaged

## Major Themes

### Social Class and Marriage
- Explores the constraints of social hierarchy in Regency England
- Critiques marriages based solely on financial security versus those based on mutual respect and love

### Pride and Prejudice
- Both main characters must overcome their flaws: Darcy's pride and Elizabeth's prejudice
- Shows how first impressions can be misleading

### Women's Limited Options
- Highlights the precarious position of women who could not inherit property
- Explores different approaches to marriage as economic necessity versus personal choice

### Individual Growth
- Characters learn to look beyond surface appearances
- Emphasizes the importance of self-reflection and personal development

## Literary Significance

- **Irony and Wit**: Austen's masterful use of irony in narration and dialogue
- **Free Indirect Discourse**: Revolutionary narrative technique that gives insight into characters' thoughts
- **Social Criticism**: Sharp but gentle satire of social conventions and human folly
- **Character Development**: Complex, realistic characters who grow throughout the story

## Cultural Impact

Pride and Prejudice has remained continuously popular since its publication, inspiring countless adaptations including:
- Multiple film versions (most notably the 1995 BBC series and 2005 film)
- Modern retellings and variations
- Academic study as a foundational text in English literature

The novel's exploration of love triumphing over social barriers, combined with Austen's wit and insight into human nature, has made it a timeless classic that continues to resonate with readers today.

## Famous Opening Line

*"It is a truth universally acknowledged, that a single man in possession of a good fortune, must be in want of a wife."*

This ironic opening immediately establishes Austen's satirical tone and introduces the novel's central preoccupation with marriage and social expectations.
