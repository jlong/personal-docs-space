1 Corinthians 6:1–8

You may be fed up with Judith, but you made vows to her and those vows still mean something to God. Perhaps you do not actually intend to divorce, but only to threaten it. But even then, that is not a response of love. Threats usually come from fear and despair, not from faith, wisdom, or charity. In either case, I believe that if you pursue this path, you will regret it for the rest of your life.

Please play this out in your head.

Even in a better-case scenario, taking legal action will most likely make the situation far more difficult for you, your wife, and your children. It could result in the children being placed in other homes. And while Jenica and Alex may not be your first choice, there are worse places — much worse — where they could be placed by the state. At least right now, there is one parent who loves them and is nearby. In a worse-case scenario, you could lose access to the children completely, or they could even be harmed in foster care.

There are some lines that, once crossed, cannot be uncrossed. They can be forgiven, yes, but the consequences may live on. Tread carefully. Much of eternal importance may depend on how you carry yourself over the next few days and months.

Ask yourself why your closest counselors, your parents, and your friends are all recommending patience. Are all of us wrong? Your situation is serious, but I do not think it is so exceptional that you should ignore the counsel of those closest to you. You have other options.

“Without counsel plans fail, but with many advisers they succeed.” Proverbs 15:22

“Plans are established by counsel; by wise guidance wage war.” Proverbs 20:18

“My son, do not lose sight of these—keep sound wisdom and discretion, and they will be life for your soul and adornment for your neck. Then you will walk on your way securely, and your foot will not stumble.” Proverbs 3:21–23

I am saying all of this because I love you and want good for you, Judith, and the children. I am not trying to minimize the pain you are in. I just want to urge you, as strongly as I can, to slow down, listen to counsel, and not take steps that may be impossible to undo.

—John
