# The Last Library

The dust motes danced in the amber light filtering through the cracked windows of what had once been the Grand Metropolitan Library. Elena ran her fingers along the spine of a leather-bound volume, its title worn smooth by countless hands that would never touch it again.

She was the last librarian in a world that had forgotten the weight of paper, the whisper of turning pages, the musty perfume of aging books. The neural implants had made reading obsolete—why struggle with words when knowledge could be downloaded directly into consciousness?

But Elena remembered the magic that the others had lost. She remembered the way stories could transport you, not just to other places, but to other selves. The way a perfectly crafted sentence could stop time, make your heart skip, change everything.

A soft chime echoed through the empty halls. Another building scheduled for demolition. Elena smiled sadly and opened the book in her hands—a collection of poetry from the early 21st century. She began to read aloud to the empty air, her voice carrying verses that would soon exist nowhere but in the vast digital void.

"Two roads diverged in a yellow wood..." she began, and for a moment, the library felt alive again.
