## Moby Dick Summary

Herman Melville's *Moby Dick* follows Ishmael, a sailor who joins Captain Ahab's whaling ship Pequod. Ahab is obsessed with hunting the white whale that took his leg. The crew embarks on what becomes a vengeful quest rather than a typical whaling voyage. After encounters with other ships and tales of the whale's power, the final confrontation destroys the Pequod and kills everyone except Ishmael. The novel explores themes of obsession, man vs. nature, and fate while serving as both an adventure story and philosophical meditation on human nature.
