Just a storybook story!

Maya's footsteps echoed through the empty corridors of the abandoned library. Dust motes danced in the pale afternoon light streaming through cracked windows, and the musty smell of forgotten books filled her nostrils.

She hadn't expected to find anything here—the city had been evacuated three years ago when the storms became too violent to predict. But as she rounded the corner into the main reading hall, her breath caught.

There, at a mahogany desk beneath a towering shelf of leather-bound volumes, sat an elderly man. He looked up from his book with eyes that twinkled like stars.

"Ah," he said, adjusting his wire-rimmed glasses. "I've been waiting for someone to find this place."

"You've been here all this time?" Maya asked, her voice barely a whisper.

He smiled and gestured to the thousands of books surrounding them. "Someone had to keep the stories safe. They're more precious than people realize—each one a doorway to another world, another possibility."

The man stood slowly, his joints creaking like old floorboards. "I'm the last librarian, and these books need a new guardian. The storms are ending soon, and people will return. When they do, they'll need these stories more than ever."

He held out a brass key, worn smooth by countless hands. "Will you help them find their way to wonder again?"

Maya reached for the key, feeling its weight—not just of metal, but of responsibility, of hope, of all the dreams waiting on dusty shelves.

Outside, thunder rumbled in the distance, but for the first time in years, it sounded like applause.
