# Frankenstein - Novel Summary

**Author:** Mary Shelley  
**Published:** 1818  
**Genre:** Gothic Fiction, Science Fiction, Horror  
**Setting:** 18th century Europe (Switzerland, Germany, England, Scotland, Ireland)

## Plot Summary

**Frankenstein** tells the story of Victor Frankenstein, a young Swiss scientist who becomes obsessed with creating life. The novel is structured as a frame narrative, beginning with Captain Robert Walton writing letters to his sister about his Arctic expedition, where he encounters Victor Frankenstein.

Victor recounts his story: As a university student passionate about natural philosophy and chemistry, he discovers the secret of creating life. Working in secret, he assembles a creature from body parts and animates it through scientific means. However, horrified by his creation's appearance, Victor abandons the creature immediately after bringing it to life.

The abandoned creature, intelligent and eloquent but hideous and lonely, teaches himself to read and speak by observing human society. Rejected by everyone he encounters due to his appearance, the creature becomes bitter and vengeful. He demands that Victor create a female companion for him, threatening to make Victor's life miserable if he refuses.

The creature begins systematically destroying Victor's life, murdering his younger brother William, framing the family servant Justine (who is executed for the crime), and later killing Victor's best friend Clerval and his bride Elizabeth on their wedding night. In despair and consumed by guilt, Victor pursues the creature to the Arctic, where he dies from exhaustion. The creature, finding Victor dead, expresses remorse and vows to destroy himself.

## Key Characters

- **Victor Frankenstein**: The ambitious scientist whose obsession with creating life leads to tragedy
- **The Creature/Monster**: Victor's creation, intelligent and articulate but driven to violence by rejection and loneliness
- **Robert Walton**: Arctic explorer who frames the narrative through his letters
- **Elizabeth Lavenza**: Victor's adopted sister and fiancée, ultimately killed by the creature
- **Henry Clerval**: Victor's loyal best friend, murdered by the creature
- **Alphonse Frankenstein**: Victor's father
- **William Frankenstein**: Victor's youngest brother, the creature's first victim

## Major Themes

**The Dangers of Unchecked Ambition**: Victor's obsessive pursuit of knowledge and power leads to destruction for himself and those he loves.

**Responsibility of Creation**: The novel explores the moral obligations a creator has toward their creation, particularly relevant as science advanced.

**Nature vs. Nurture**: The creature's transformation from innocent to vengeful raises questions about whether evil is inherent or learned.

**Isolation and Alienation**: Both Victor and his creature suffer from profound loneliness that drives their destructive behavior.

**The Sublime and Natural World**: Shelley contrasts the beauty and power of nature with human ambition and scientific hubris.

## Literary Significance

- **Birth of Science Fiction**: Often considered one of the first true science fiction novels
- **Gothic Tradition**: Exemplifies Gothic literature with its dark themes, supernatural elements, and psychological horror
- **Romantic Movement**: Reflects Romantic ideals about nature, emotion, and the dangers of excessive rationalism
- **Feminist Perspective**: Written by an 18-year-old woman, it offers a unique voice in male-dominated literary discourse

## Cultural Impact

The novel has become a cornerstone of popular culture, spawning countless adaptations, films, and reinterpretations. The name "Frankenstein" has entered common usage to describe any creation that turns against its creator. The story continues to resonate in discussions about scientific ethics, artificial intelligence, and genetic engineering, making it remarkably prescient for a work written over 200 years ago.
